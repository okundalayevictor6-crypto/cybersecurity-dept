Student Policies & Regulations

All students are expected to adhere to the following rules and regulations as outlined in the student handbook.

General

All students must dress corporate except in halls of residence or during sports. Appearance must be neat, pure, and simple, conforming to Christian values. Tattooing is prohibited. Face caps are not allowed in lecture halls.

Female Students

  • No sexually provocative dressing (chest, navel, thigh covered).
  • Faces fully visible.
  • Skirts must cover knees, not slit above.
  • No transparent/sheer blouses.
  • No low necklines; shoulders covered.
  • No shorts/slacks outside residence (except field trips).
  • Trouser suits allowed with modest camisole.
  • No excessive makeup, multi‑coloured nails, artificial nails, bleaching.
  • Hair attachments not beyond neck‑length, natural colour.
  • Jewelry moderate; no noisy shoes.

Male Students

  • Attire for lectures: trousers (not jeans), shirt and tie.
  • Not bare‑footed or shirtless.
  • Proper sports uniforms during activities.
  • No muscle tops, earrings, necklaces.
  • Hair short, neat; beard clean shaven.

Sanctions: First offence – strong reprimand; second – community service; third – rustication one semester.

Eligibility

  • 80% lecture attendance required.
  • No absence >3 weeks without permission.

Instructions to Candidates

  • University ID and Examination card required.
  • No incriminating materials on person or desk.
  • No GSM or electronic devices in hall.
  • No leaving during first and last 30 minutes.
  • Only non‑programmable calculators allowed.
  • Silence must be observed.

Examination Misconduct

Offences include possession of unauthorized materials, swapping answers, impersonation, assault, etc. Penalties range from reprimand to expulsion.

  • Respect for university officials at all times.
  • No disrespectful or insulting letters.
  • Orderly behaviour in classrooms, library, and around campus.
  • Adherence to library rules (silence, no food, bag inspection).
  • Registration and matriculation mandatory.
  • Any student who writes an insulting letter to an official is subject to disciplinary action.
  • Every student must register with the University Health Centre.
  • For any ailment, report at the University Health Centre first.
  • Only the Director's report is acceptable for official purposes.
  • Outside treatment must be endorsed by the Director.
  • The university must be informed of any sickness during the session.
  • Silence must be observed; all handsets on silent.
  • No library material taken out without signing.
  • All materials must be returned promptly.
  • No food or drink allowed.
  • Bags and books inspected at security post when leaving.
  • Library ID required for entry.
  • Personal belongings (except purses, notebooks) deposited at property counter.
  • GPA < 1.50 in one semester → probation.
  • Two consecutive semesters with GPA < 1.50 → withdrawal from program.
  • CGPA < 1.00 in two consecutive sessions → withdrawal from university.
  • A student asked to withdraw may transfer to another programme if minimum requirements are met.
