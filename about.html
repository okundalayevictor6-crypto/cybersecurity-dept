About the Department

A brief history, our philosophy, and what makes our Cyber Security programme unique.

Our History

The Cyber Security programme was established in 2015 as an arm of the Department of Physical Sciences at Chrisland University. Over the years, it has evolved into a distinct programme within the Department of Computer Sciences, producing highly trained students in the field of Cyber Security. The curriculum meets the National Universities Commission (NUC) Benchmark for Minimum Academic Standards and complies with global requirements for a degree in Cyber Security.

Staff members are at the forefront of computer research, enabling the department to train quality students who occupy various positions in banks, industries, academia, and private companies.

Philosophy & Objectives

Philosophy:

To evolve a broad strategy for human resource development that encompasses educational, cultural, social and political development such that graduates can possess the unique cyber security ability to protect digital assets, guard critical infrastructure, and business ecosystems.

Aim:

To build capacity and develop human capital in the field of cyber security, and to safeguard business transactions as well as corporate assets nationally and globally.

Specific Objectives:

  • Provide students with a broad and balanced foundation of Cyber Security knowledge and practical skills.
  • Stimulate sustained interest and enthusiasm in Cyber Security and its applications.
  • Train students in necessary computing knowledge and skills to provide effective computing service to industries, banking, and other sectors.
  • Produce professionally trained and skilled manpower to develop internationally acceptable software applications.
  • Provide basic networks and hardware knowledge needed for modern ICT practice.
  • Train students with a solid foundation for postgraduate studies and research.
  • Inculcate a culture of focused and continuous skills for carrying out research.
  • Develop transferable skills applicable to all aspects of human endeavour.
  • Prepare students for self-employment and job placements in government and industry.

Vision

To be the leading program in Research and Teaching towards the Economic Growth of the Nation through ICT.

Mission

Stimulate sustained interest and enthusiasm in Cyber security; train students with necessary computing knowledge and skills; provide solid foundation for postgraduate studies; conduct research to advance the state of the art.

Admission Requirements

UTME Entry:

Five ‘O’ Level credit passes in English, Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and any other science subject. UTME subjects: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry.

Direct Entry:

NCE in Cyber Security with relevant subjects or two ‘A’ Level passes in Physics and Mathematics or Chemistry. OND/NCE (minimum upper credit) may be considered for 200 level.

Graduation Requirements

  • All registered courses must be passed.
  • All compulsory courses taken and passed.
  • All GST courses passed.
  • Minimum total units: 144 (UTME) / 108 (Direct Entry).
  • CGPA of at least 1.50.
  • Found worthy in character.

Accreditation

The Cyber Security programme is fully accredited by the National Universities Commission (NUC) and meets global standards for a Bachelor's degree.

Facilities

State-of-the-art computer laboratories, cybersecurity simulation labs, dedicated servers, and high-speed internet access for hands-on training.
